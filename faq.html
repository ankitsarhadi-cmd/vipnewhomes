Getting Started

Before a new development opens to the public, builders offer a short window of early access to registered agents and their clients. During VIP Access, you get first pick of lots, models, and floor plans — often at pre-launch pricing before any increases. The best corner lots, largest models, and premium elevations sell out before most buyers even know the project exists. VIP registration through Ankit is free and costs you nothing.
With resale, you buy an existing home and move in within 30–90 days. With pre-construction, you're buying a home that hasn't been built yet — you sign today and take possession when construction is complete (typically 12–36 months later). The key advantages of pre-con:
  • You lock in today's price while values may rise during construction
  • Deposits are spread over time, not due all at once
  • Brand-new home — no renovations, no history
  • Full Tarion warranty coverage
  • Ability to choose finishes, upgrades, and sometimes layouts

The Purchase Process

Deposits vary by builder but typically total 15–20% of the purchase price, paid in stages over 12–24 months. A common structure:
  • 5% on signing
  • 5% at 90 days
  • 5% at 180 days
  • Balance to 20% at 365 days (if required)
This staged structure gives you time to arrange financing without a large upfront lump sum. Ankit reviews the exact deposit schedule with you before you sign.
The APS is the legal contract between you and the builder. It outlines the purchase price, deposit schedule, closing date, permitted delays, included features, and your rights under Tarion. Ankit attends every signing with you, and he connects you with a real estate lawyer to review the contract before you're bound to anything. Never sign a builder APS without legal review.
Ontario law gives you a 10-calendar-day cooling-off period after signing a new-home APS. During this time, you can cancel the agreement for any reason and receive a full refund of your deposit — no penalty. Use this window to have a lawyer review the contract thoroughly. Ankit always ensures this review happens before the window closes.

Costs & Financials

If you're purchasing as an investment property or rental, the HST rebate may not apply and the full HST can add significantly to your closing costs. Your lawyer will confirm this based on your specific situation.
Development charges (DCs) are fees municipalities impose on builders to fund infrastructure — roads, sewers, schools. Many builders cap their DCs, meaning any increases after a set date are absorbed by the builder, not passed to you. Others have no cap, meaning charges can increase between signing and closing. Ankit highlights this in every project review — it can be a significant difference between projects.
In addition to your purchase price, budget for:
  • Land Transfer Tax — provincial (and municipal in Toronto). First-time buyers receive a rebate
  • Legal fees — typically $1,500–$3,000
  • Tarion enrolment fee — paid to the builder, already included in most prices
  • Utility connections, meter installations — varies by builder
  • Adjustments — property tax, utility, and condo fee adjustments at closing

During Construction

Typically 12 to 36 months, depending on the project stage when you buy. Some communities are already under construction (shorter timeline), while others are in early planning (longer). The APS will specify an estimated closing date range. Ankit will clearly outline the expected timeline for any project before you commit.
Delays are common in construction. Ontario's Tarion regulations allow builders a set number of permitted delays (outlined in your APS) with proper written notice. If the builder delays beyond the permitted extensions, you have the right to cancel and receive your deposit back. Ankit and your lawyer monitor any delay notices and advise you on your options.
An assignment means selling your purchase contract to another buyer before you take possession. Many builders allow this, but policies and fees vary — some charge an assignment fee of $5,000–$10,000 or require builder consent. Assignment clauses must be reviewed before signing the APS. Ankit flags assignment terms as part of every deal review.

Warranty & Protection

Tarion is Ontario's government-backed new home warranty program. Every new home built by a licensed builder in Ontario is covered:
  • 1 year — defects in work and materials
  • 2 years — electrical, plumbing, heating, and building envelope
  • 7 years — major structural defects
This protection is one of the strongest arguments for buying new over resale.
Yes — Tarion's deposit protection covers up to $100,000 for freehold homes and condos in Ontario. This means if your builder becomes insolvent before closing, your deposit is protected up to that limit. Ankit only works with established, reputable builders to minimize this risk from the outset.
